With “[Mina Navigators](https://minafoundation.notion.site/Mina-Navigators-Program-Information-9e8de53ce8174db7b0aea687ccc9465e)” the intention is to provide monthly funding for projects over a period of 6 months that are intended to add value to the Mina community. In our recent discussions, [Will](https://twitter.com/WillCove) from Mina Foundation has repeatedly referred to Kevin Kelly’s blog post, “[Scenius, or Communal Genius](https://kk.org/thetechnium/scenius-or-comm/)”. Here is an excerpt:

“The serendipitous ingredients for scenius are hard to control. They depend on the presence of the right early pioneers. A place that is open, but not too open. A buffer that is tolerant of outlaws. And some flash of excitement to kick off the virtuous circle. You just can’t order this.” 🌠

In this post, I am trying to work out for myself what foundations a project must meet to be perceived from my perspective as a “high-quality” or complete node and to achieve the desired group effect. 🚀

A node (project) must speak the same language as the other nodes in the network. This can refer to a programming language but also to certain expected behaviors to enable seamless integration:

1. **Open-Source Standard:** The standard is open source, and only in a few exceptional cases might a closed-source solution be necessary. In such cases, it should be outsourced to a separate repository to keep as much code open as possible. 📦

2. **Standardized Integration: **Projects should be modular and integratable via package managers. This step signals the group’s interest in collaborative exchange. The same value should be placed on straightforward integration for protocols. 🛠️

3. **API Design:** Interfaces should be defined as broadly as possible to enable a wide range of applications without compromising the core purpose. A clean abstraction reduces complexity. 🔄

4. **Automated Testing:** To be compatible with the group, staging-level quality is initially sufficient. Suitability for production can be pursued but requires additional steps that would otherwise slow down the initial release. 🧪

5. **Complexity Reduction: **To quickly navigate a foreign repository, attention should be paid to a simple folder structure, clear separation of tests, and a uniform documentation format. 📂

6. **Documentation: **Documentation can generally be divided into “Reference” and the actual documentation. The goal is to have self-contained documentation to make searches as straightforward as possible. 📖

All of the above points are important and should be implemented in some way. The timing of when “Mina Navigators” begins to fund projects through the program is a matter of interpretation. This includes, of course, the actual purpose and content of the software.

“A place that is open, but not too open.” 🏛️

In case of doubt, I would argue that projects clearly demonstrating their intent to connect with other projects should be given preference. 💡🤝

I want to emphasize that these are only my thoughts on the matter. The actual criteria for consideration for Mina Navigators can be found here: [https://minafoundation.notion.site/Hackathon-criteria-for-winning-a-Navigator-badge-7f4229fd7e6942e2bfd046a50e7864a1](https://minafoundation.notion.site/Hackathon-criteria-for-winning-a-Navigator-badge-7f4229fd7e6942e2bfd046a50e7864a1).
